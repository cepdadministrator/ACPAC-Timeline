Adult/Paediatric Practitioner in Rheumatology Program

Advanced Clinician Practitioner in Arthritis Care (ACPAC) Program founded at St. Michael's Hospital in collaboration with the Hospital for Sick Children, Toronto, Canada.

Established the first interprofessional, formal academic and clinical curriculum based program in advanced arthritis care offered at the post licensure level to experienced Physical Therapists and Occupational Therapists from across Ontario accredited through the Department of Continuing Professional Development, Faculty of Medicine, University of Toronto. The ACPAC Program is multiprofessional, transinstitutional, involving members from the Faculty of Medicine, U of T and community based experts in arthritis care across Ontario

The ACPAC program was designed as a joint collaborative venture involving key stakeholders including academia, The Arthritis Society and Industry.

2012-2013

ACPAC Program Transitional Year

This year aimed to yield an enhanced academic liason with the Ministry of Health, Human Health Resource Division and the Department of Continuing Education and Professional Development, Faculty of Medicine, University of Toronto to support a further 5 Year funding of the ACPAC program.

Newly designed website: www.chronicdiseases.ca/arthritis

The ACPAC Program Curriculum was re-designed to include 5 on-site weeks with off-site clinical training between September-June :

Highlights of change included:

  • The program was modified in format to include an online component, off-site clinical training and a reduction to five weeks of on-site attendance.
  • Applicants may be physical therapists, occupational therapists, or nurses.
  • Candidates may apply from across Canada with the proviso that geo- graphic and regional distribution will need to be taken into consideration. A core group of applicants will continue to be from Ontario.
